Living Life on Purpose

As a Christian, living life on purpose is deeply rooted in your faith and relationship with God. Here are some ways to live life on purpose as a Christian:

  1. Seek God's Will: Regularly seek God's guidance through prayer, meditation on His Word, and seeking wise counsel. Invite Him to reveal His purpose for your life and guide your decisions.
  2. Align with Biblical Principles: Base your values, goals, and actions on biblical principles. Let God's Word be your guide in every area of life, including relationships, work, finances, and decision-making.
  3. Serve Others: Follow Jesus' example of servant leadership by looking for opportunities to serve others with love and compassion. Use your gifts, talents, and resources to bless and uplift those around you.
  4. Share Your Faith: Live out your faith boldly and authentically, sharing the hope and love of Christ with others. Look for opportunities to share your testimony, invite others to church or Bible study, and engage in conversations about faith.
  5. Practice Forgiveness: Forgive others as Christ has forgiven you. Let go of bitterness, resentment, and anger, and extend grace and forgiveness to those who have wronged you.
  6. Grow Spiritually: Commit to growing spiritually through regular Bible study, worship, fellowship with other believers, and participation in spiritual disciplines like fasting and prayer. Seek to deepen your relationship with God and become more like Christ.
  7. Stewardship: Recognize that everything you have belongs to God, and practice good stewardship of your time, talents, finances, and resources. Use them wisely and for God's glory.
  8. Live with Hope: Anchor your life in the hope of eternal life with God. Let this hope motivate you to live faithfully, persevere through challenges, and trust in God's promises.

By living out your faith in these ways, you can experience a purposeful and fulfilling life as a Christian, bringing glory to God and blessing others along the way.

Selfish Deeds

Galatians 5:19-21 provides a detailed list of behaviours and attitudes that are contrary to the Christian life. These include:

  1. Sexual Immorality: Any form of sexual activity outside the bounds of marriage between a man and a woman.
  2. Impurity: Moral uncleanness or actions that defile one's character or integrity.
  3. Lustful Pleasures: Pursuing selfish desires and indulging in pleasures that are contrary to God's will.
  4. Idolatry: Worshiping anything or anyone other than God, placing created things above the Creator.
  5. Sorcery: Involvement in occult practices or seeking spiritual power outside of God's authority.
  6. Hatred: Deep-seated animosity and hostility towards others.
  7. Quarreling: Engaging in heated arguments and disputes that lead to division and discord.
  8. Jealousy: Envious resentment towards others' success, possessions, or blessings.
  9. Outbursts of Anger: Uncontrolled rage and explosive reactions to situations or people.
  10. Selfish Ambition: Pursuing personal gain and status at the expense of others.
  11. Dissension: Causing division, strife, and conflict within the Christian community.
  12. Factions: Forming cliques or groups that promote division and exclusivity.
  13. Envy: Coveting what others have and desiring to possess it for oneself.
  14. Drunkenness: Excessive consumption of alcohol leading to loss of self-control and judgment.
  15. Orgies: Participation in wild and unrestrained parties or gatherings that promote sinful behaviour.

The passage concludes by warning that those who practice such things will not inherit the kingdom of God.

The Fruit of the Spirit

Galatians 5:22-23 highlights the fruits of the Spirit, which are the desirable qualities and characteristics that should be evident in the life of a Christian:

  1. Love: Agape love, is characterized by selflessness, compassion, and genuine concern for others.
  2. Joy: A deep-seated gladness and contentment that comes from knowing God and His promises.
  3. Peace: Inner tranquillity and harmony, regardless of external circumstances, rooted in trust in God.
  4. Patience: Endurance and forbearance in difficult situations, showing restraint and tolerance towards others.
  5. Kindness: Acts of goodness, generosity, and benevolence towards others, reflecting God's loving nature.
  6. Goodness: Moral excellence and integrity, doing what is right and just in all situations.
  7. Faithfulness: Loyalty, reliability, and trustworthiness in keeping promises and commitments.
  8. Gentleness: Humility, meekness, and considerate behaviour towards others, avoiding harshness or aggression.
  9. Self-Control: Mastery over one's desires, impulses, and actions, exercising discipline and moderation.

These fruits of the Spirit are evidence of a transformed life guided by the Holy Spirit. They enable believers to reflect the character of Christ and bear witness to His love and grace in the world.

True Nature of Discipleship

In Matthew 7:21-23, Jesus delivers a sobering message about the true nature of discipleship. He emphasizes that mere verbal acknowledgment of Him as Lord is insufficient for entering the kingdom of heaven. Instead, genuine discipleship is marked by obedience to the will of God. This passage underscores the importance of a sincere, transformative relationship with God, one that reflects His values and aligns with His purposes. It warns against relying solely on external actions or religious practices, stressing the necessity of authentic faith expressed through obedience and a heart surrendered to God.

Furthermore, Jesus highlights the danger of self-deception within religious contexts. He portrays a scenario where individuals claim to have performed miraculous deeds and spiritual acts in His name. Despite their outward displays of religious fervor, Jesus reveals that their hearts were not aligned with God's will. This serves as a cautionary tale, urging believers to examine the authenticity of their faith and ensure that it is rooted in a genuine relationship with God rather than superficial acts or religious rituals. The passage challenges us to prioritize a deep, intimate connection with God and live out our faith in obedience and sincerity.
